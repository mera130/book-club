Honey bees Honey bees (Apis species)(Apis species) are among the most important pollinators in the world, playing a crucial role in ecosystems and agriculture. A typical honey bee colony consists of three types of bees: the queen, worker bees, and drones. The queen is the sole fertile female in the hive and can lay up to 2,000 eggs per day. Worker bees, which are sterile females, perform all the essential tasks such as foraging, building the hive, feeding larvae, and protecting the colony. Male drones exist only to mate with a queen, and they die shortly after mating. If they do not mate, worker bees often expel them from the hive before winter.

why are they important

Honey bees are best known for their ability to produce honey. They collect nectar from flowers using their long tongues and store it in their honey stomachs. Once back at the hive, they pass the nectar to other bees, who break it down into simple sugars and evaporate excess water by fanning it with their wings, eventually turning it into honey. This honey serves as a food source for the colony, especially during colder months when foraging is not possible.
